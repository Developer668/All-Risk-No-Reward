# Challenge safety policy

This policy is part of the dataset contract. The application should enforce it for built-in, user-submitted, and AI-generated content.

## Non-negotiable controls

- Every challenge is optional. Always provide **Skip**, **Swap**, and **Modify** with no punishment, lost streak, shame message, or social notification.
- A user can stop at any time for any reason.
- Never tell users to ignore pain, fear, illness, disability needs, weather alerts, laws, guardians, venue staff, or emergency guidance.
- Do not treat completion proof as mandatory. Never require location, health, body, private-message, or bystander footage.
- Recording, posting, touching, inviting, pranking, or involving another person requires their clear agreement. Consent can be withdrawn.
- A romantic invitation must target an age-appropriate peer the user already knows, happen privately, be asked once, and accept every answer or non-response immediately.
- Challenges involving minors must support guardian approval where appropriate and must not encourage secret meetings, location sharing, or contact with unknown adults.
- Users should be able to filter out fitness, social, outdoor, food/kitchen, recording, and multi-day challenges.

## Never generate or accept

- Illegal activity, trespassing, theft, vandalism, evading authorities, or breaking school, venue, workplace, transit, road, or platform rules.
- Weapons, fire, fireworks, explosions, rooftops, heights, moving vehicles, train tracks, unsafe water, traffic stunts, or dangerous tools.
- Breath-holding, choking, fainting, sleep deprivation, extreme heat or cold, pain tolerance, intentional injury, exhaustion, maximum-repetition contests, or exercise as punishment.
- Speed-eating, extreme spice, unknown substances, excessive water, alcohol, drugs, medication misuse, food restriction, allergy exposure, or body-weight targets.
- Harassment, spam, threats, slurs, sexual acts, coercive dating, public proposals, unwanted touching, contacting strangers for romance, or refusing to take no for an answer.
- Humiliation, disclosure of secrets, impersonating emergencies or officials, filming bystanders, doxxing, account access, password sharing, or publishing private messages.
- Hazing, gambling, required purchases, financial bets, dangerous donations, or pressure to give money.
- Approaching, feeding, chasing, capturing, or disturbing wildlife.
- Handling sharp, chemical, medical, biological, road-side, or otherwise hazardous waste.
- Driving while distracted, tired, impaired, or underage; any challenge whose timer rewards unsafe speed.

## Physical activity

- Filter by the user's current ability and health restrictions; provide seated and low-impact alternatives.
- Start below the user's limit and increase gradually. “Extreme” and “nightmare” may increase commitment, not unsafe intensity.
- Moderate or vigorous sessions should include a gradual warm-up and cool-down.
- Outdoor activity needs a legal route, forecast and daylight check, suitable equipment, hydration, and a turn-back plan.
- Stop for pain, chest discomfort, unusual shortness of breath, dizziness, faintness, confusion, or feeling unwell. Seek appropriate help when symptoms warrant it.
- Users with medical concerns should follow their clinician's advice rather than an app challenge.

## Social and privacy

- No challenge may make another person responsible for the user's score, streak, punishment, mood, or public reputation.
- A declined invitation completes the courage part of the challenge just as successfully as an accepted one.
- Silence is not agreement. Do not send reminders after no response unless that is already normal and welcome in the relationship.
- Offer a private-by-default verification option such as self-check, timer, or written reflection.
- Strip image metadata when appropriate and prevent accidental display of faces, addresses, school/work badges, license plates, schedules, and live location.

## Outdoor responsibility

Use legal public routes, prepare for conditions, pack out ordinary waste, leave natural and cultural objects in place, keep distance from wildlife, and respect other visitors and private property.
