## Design Context

### Users

People who want structured practice with social confidence and inhibition, especially users who experience avoidance or mild social anxiety. They primarily use the product on a phone throughout an ordinary day: checking the daily challenge, completing a short real-world action, and submitting private proof or reflection.

### Brand Personality

Audacious, warm, and candid. The interface should make discomfort feel adventurous and achievable, never clinical, punitive, manipulative, or shaming. It should create curiosity in the morning and genuine celebration after an attempt.

### Aesthetic Direction

A light, editorial “sunrise field guide meets arcade challenge deck” aesthetic with warm paper, punchy red-orange, ink blue, physical scorecard details, and expressive typography. The product should feel authored and energetic rather than like a generic SaaS dashboard. Avoid purple technology gradients, glassmorphism, interchangeable rounded cards, sterile wellness branding, and aggressive punishment imagery.

### Design Principles

1. Agency before engagement: boundaries, resizing, and private recovery are always visible and understandable.
2. Reward attempts and concrete progress, not embarrassment or compliance.
3. Use surprise to interrupt avoidance without hiding consequences or removing user control.
4. Protect the privacy and dignity of both the user and anyone involved in a challenge.
5. Make the daily challenge the singular visual event; supporting metrics remain secondary.
